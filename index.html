About
Rino Bellocco
Professor of Biostatistics · Milano-Bicocca & Karolinska Institutet
RG KI

I am a Professor of Biostatistics at the Department of Statistics and Quantitative Methods (DISMEQ) at the University of Milano-Bicocca since October 2016, and I work part-time as a Visiting Professor in the Biostatistics Group in the Department of Medical Epidemiology and Biostatistics (MEB), Karolinska Institutet in Stockholm, where I moved to in November 1998 after earning my PhD from Harvard University.

My first research interest — at the Italian National Institute of Health and then at the Harvard School of Public Health — was in the epidemiology of infectious diseases, and in particular in the mathematical modelling of transmission dynamics of HIV infection, with the implementation and application of statistical models for predicting the future number of AIDS cases.

My primary research is now in the field of lifestyle epidemiology, applying both standard statistical models and developing causal models for confounding and mediation to study the aetiology of many chronic diseases, ranging from cardiovascular and cancer outcomes to cause-specific (breast cancer, ovarian cancer, prostate cancer) and overall mortality.

I am co-PI of the Swedish National March Cohort (SNMC), based on almost 50,000 individuals enrolled during the Cancer Day in September 1997. I am currently the coordinator of the Italian Working Group on Causal Inference in Epidemiology (ICE), together with Dr. Lorenzo Richiardi (University of Turin).

I am the coordinator of the Erasmus and International exchange programme for the MSc in Biostatistics, and the organiser of the Seminar Series in Biostatistics, Epidemiology and Public Health at the University of Milano-Bicocca.
